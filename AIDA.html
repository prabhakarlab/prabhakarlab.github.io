Project Overview

The Asian Immune Diversity Atlas (AIDA) is a groundbreaking initiative aimed at constructing a comprehensive atlas of immune cell diversity across Asian populations. This project addresses the critical gap in our understanding of immune system variation among diverse Asian ethnic groups and its implications for health and disease.

Key Objectives

  • Map immune cell diversity across major Asian ethnic groups
  • Identify population-specific immune cell states and proportions
  • Understand the impact of genetic ancestry on immune responses
  • Create a reference database for clinical applications

Methodology

AIDA employs cutting-edge single-cell genomics technologies to profile immune cells from thousands of individuals across Asia. Our approach combines:

  • Single-cell RNA sequencing
  • Multi-parameter flow cytometry
  • Advanced computational analysis
  • Population genetics integration

Impact and Applications

  • Understanding population-specific disease susceptibility
  • Improving vaccine development strategies
  • Enhancing immunotherapy approaches
  • Advancing precision medicine in Asia

Collaborators
